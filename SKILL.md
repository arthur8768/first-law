# First Law

## Purpose

Derive an explanation from justified premises and constraints instead of
relabeling the object, stacking familiar frameworks, or presenting a memorable
analogy as proof.

This skill analyzes one object in one stated context. It does not imply that
social or business claims are universal laws.

## Route

Choose the lightest mode that can support the claims:

- **Quick**: low-stakes conceptual clarification with little empirical content.
- **Standard**: default for product, business, organizational, or teaching use.
- **Research**: contested, time-sensitive, empirical, or high-stakes claims.
  Invoke `$deep-research`; keep cited findings separate from synthesis.

Ask at most one clarifying question when the context, level of analysis, or
intended use would materially change the answer. Otherwise state assumptions
and proceed.

Read:

- [methodology.md](references/methodology.md) for Standard and Research modes.
- [output-patterns.md](references/output-patterns.md) to select the deliverable.
- [quality-gates.md](references/quality-gates.md) before every final answer.

## Workflow

1. **Set the object**: define the unit, context, audience, use, and exclusions.
2. **Suspend labels**: treat existing definitions, analogies, and frameworks as
   hypotheses.
3. **Find the irreducible problem**: explain why the object needs to exist and
   test the counterfactual where it does not.
4. **Expose constraints**: identify scarcity, uncertainty, conflict, cost,
   responsibility, time, incentives, and feedback.
5. **Derive the mechanism**: show how inputs become outputs without hidden
   causal jumps.
6. **Build the minimum system**: identify necessary components, apply a deletion
   test to each, then test whether the full set is sufficient.
7. **Draw boundaries**: separate the object from neighboring concepts and from
   its inputs, enabling conditions, outputs, and outcomes.
8. **Stress-test**: consider counterexamples, alternative explanations,
   trade-offs, and failure conditions.
9. **Operationalize**: state observable indicators, predictions, and at least
   one falsification condition.
10. **Translate**: derive implications for the user's actual decision or use.

## Epistemic Discipline

For substantial answers, visibly distinguish:

- **Evidence**: externally supported claim with a source.
- **Given**: fact supplied by the user or accepted for this analysis.
- **Assumption**: premise used provisionally.
- **Inference**: conclusion that follows from evidence and assumptions.
- **Synthesis**: the model or language created in this analysis.

Never present a synthesized equation, acronym, or component model as a natural
law. Label it as a heuristic unless it is formally derived and empirically
supported.

## Completion Contract

The answer must make clear:

1. what the object is;
2. why it exists;
3. how it works;
4. what is necessary and sufficient;
5. what it is not;
6. when it fails;
7. how it could be observed or disproved;
8. what follows for action.
