# Output Patterns

Select one pattern and adapt it to the user's language and intended use. Do not
fill sections with repetition merely to preserve the template.

## Quick Conceptual Answer

Use for low-stakes clarification.

```markdown
## Direct Answer
[Strict definition]

In plain language: [one-sentence explanation]

## Why It Exists
[Irreducible problem and counterfactual]

## Minimum Model
[Necessary components and one-line deletion result for each]

## Key Boundary
[What it is not and the most important neighboring distinction]

## Test
[One observable prediction or falsification condition]

## Implication
[One non-obvious consequence]
```

## Evidence-Backed Research Analysis

Use when claims are empirical, contested, time-sensitive, or high-stakes.
Invoke `$deep-research` and preserve its source-quality requirements.

```markdown
## Evidence-Calibrated Answer
[Conclusion, evidence strength, and key uncertainty]

## Definition Landscape
| Source or school | Definition | Unit of analysis | Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|

## Source-Backed Findings
[Only claims directly supported by cited sources]

## First-Principles Synthesis
[Derived model, explicitly labeled as synthesis]

## Competing Models And Tensions
[Disagreements, boundary choices, and evidence that could change the conclusion]

## Operational Model
[Minimum system, indicators, prediction, falsification]

## Practical Implications
[Actions linked to evidence or stated assumptions]

## Limitations
[Coverage, transferability, measurement, and freshness limits]
```

Citation rules:

- Place citations beside the claims they support.
- Do not cite a source for a stronger claim than it makes.
- Prefer primary research, standards, official data, and authoritative records.
- Label domain transfer as inference.
- Do not convert absence of evidence into evidence of absence.

## Teaching Or Content Translation

Use only after the analysis is complete.

Produce:

1. one counterintuitive title;
2. one familiar misconception;
3. a three-to-six-part explanatory model;
4. one concrete scenario;
5. one self-check or exercise;
6. one sentence about limits.

Keep two layers distinct:

- **Analytical model:** precise, qualified, testable.
- **Communication model:** memorable simplification.

Never let the communication model silently replace the analytical model.

## Useful Closing Forms

### Strict Definition

```text
In [context], X is the [type of thing] that performs [irreducible function]
through [mechanism] under [constraints], producing [observable output].
```

### Plain-Language Definition

```text
X is not [common confusion]. It is what allows [actor] to [essential function]
when [binding difficulty].
```

### Non-Obvious Insight

```text
The scarce part is not [visible activity], but [binding mechanism or
constraint], because [causal reason].
```
